Database of detentions at public protests 2011-2020

This dataset represents one of the outcomes of monitoring politically motivated detentions carried out by the OVD-Info team from 2011 to 2020.

By Jean Michel Shcherbak

November, 10 2023

Arrests or detentions in the context of criminal or administrative proceedings, even if they are linked to the political activities of an activist, are not included in this dataset. Credit Igor Ivanko, Kommersant.

Election — protests opposing election manipulation, rallies advocating for candidates' access to elections, and similar activities.

Gender rights — events promoting gender equality, feminist initiatives, and LGBTQ rights protests.

Against the government — numerous protest actions may lack a distinct topic but carry a clear political (anti-Putin, anti-governor, anti-Medvedev) element. We have introduced this category for such instances.

The political motive behind a detention is determined by the circumstances: it can involve arresting someone at a political event, detaining a political activist during campaigning or poster distribution, or an unwarranted arrest of a person known for their political or social activism. Here, 'politics' is broadly understood as advocating for one's rights and interests — events related to environmentalism, LGBTQ rights, urban development, or social demands are considered political by OVD-Info

"Under the term 'action,' we refer to public events, primarily street protests like rallies, marches, and pickets. Public lectures, festivals, performances, and concerts are also considered actions, even if they are not street-based. Finally, political agitation, such as distributing leaflets on the streets or posting posters in the metro, is also regarded as an action for our dataset*," said Maria Chashilovaб – a lawyer and human rights activist from Moscow.

*Arrest or detention in the context of criminal or administrative proceedings, even if they are linked to the political activities of an activist, are not included in this dataset.
